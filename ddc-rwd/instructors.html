Meet our Staff!

Our studio is dedicated to you and your child's safety. Our team is a group of well-known dance instructors and part of our community. Our studio has always remained a safe place for your child to dance and feel comfortable.

Hillary Wagher- Co-Director
A Norwich native, Hillary attended Manhattanville College on a performing arts merit scholarship and graduated Summa Cum Laude with the Capezio Award. While at Manhattanville she was a principle dancer with the Manhattanville Dance Ensemble, the first U.S. College Dance Ensemble to perform in Leningrad prior to the breakup of the Soviet Union. Hillary has studied with such greats as Ann Carlson, Luigi, Phil Black, Irene Feigenheimer, Gus Solomons Jr., David Storey, Gemze de Lappe and privately with Annabelle Gamson.

Chris Guerin - Co-Director
A Plainfield native, Chris has been active in theater and dance most of her life. She studied drama at Tisch School of Arts at NYU, and danced with Lee Theodore's American Dance Machine while attending NYU. She received numerous awards in such competitions as Show Stoppers and Dance Masters of America. Chris has studied with gretas such as Frank Hatchett, Phil Black and Tommy Tune. Chris is active in both performance and production aspects of community theater.

Sara Kessler - Instructor
Sara is originally from Colchester CT, and is a 2010 graduate of Dean College. She has performed professionally with both the Albano Ballet Company and the Pioneer Valley Ballet Company in numerous productions. She is the Dance Department Head at the Nathan Hale Arts Magnet School. Sara is also the director of the award winning Bacon Academy Dance Team.

Kayla Thomas- Instructor
Kayla is a graduate of Rhode Island College with a double major in Dance and Social Work. At Danceworks she teaches Hip Hop, Jazz, Introduction to Dance and Introduction to Tumbling. Kayla became a part of the Danceworks family at age four and never left! She performed with the Repertory Company for six years earning many regional and national awards. She served as a student assistant during high school and as a student teacher during her early college years. Kayla's love of dance and children shows in her exciting choreography and fun classes.

Keri Danner - Instructor
Keri is well known for her work with local community theater, especially high school drama programs. Keri studied dance with the Luis Pabon Dance Artrs Center where she earned a national championship title. She has worked with KCT, Break-a-Leg Productions, Hartford's Children's Theater, Griswold HS, Plainfield HS, Woodstock Academy and the Bradley Playhouse. Her choreography of "The Wizard of Oz" and "Guys and Dolls" was nominated for excellence by the CT High School Music Awards Committee. At Danceworks Keri teaches Ballet, Tap, Jazz, Introduction to Dance and Musical Theater.

Courtney Klewin- Instructor
Danceworks is proud to welcome Acro teacher Courtney Klewin. She has a thirteen year history of award winning competitive gymnastics at local, regional and national levels. Courtney is a Canterbury native and a graduate of Griswold HS. Her work experience includes teaching at Deary's Gymnastics, Flip City Gymnastics, and Play and Learn Child Development Center where she is the head teacher for the pre-school program and coordinates the summer camp program.

Jessica Garvie - Instructor
Jessica began her dance training the year Danceworks opened at the age of 8 and has been a major part of the studio ever since. While a member of the Repertory Co. Jessica became known as a strong performer and a creative choreographer and has won many awards at competitions with the Danceworks Repertory Company. Jessica is a graduate of Plainfield HS and of Eastern CT State University with a degree in early childhood education. Students in her dance classes know she is passionate about working with children. Jessica is currently a pre-sechool teacher at Putnam Elementary School.

Dedicated to Your Success

Knowing that experience and exposure to the opportunities of performance is key to the development of a dancer's training, we offer many such opportunities for our students by performing at many local and community events. Collaborative efforts of Danceworks include choreography for several local theater groups including the Norwich Arts Council, Plainfield High School Drama, and The Theater of Northeastern Connecticut at The Bradley Playhouse. The annual PIMA pageant for the town of Plainfield has become an established tradition in their spring schedule.
